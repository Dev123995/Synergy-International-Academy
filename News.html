INVESTITURE CEREMONY - 2024-25

Sri Prakash Synergy School recently held its prestigious Investiture Ceremony at the Sita Narasimha multi-Purpose Auditorium, graced by Dr. Anoosha Choudhary Murakonda as the Guest of Honour. The event showcased a vibrant parade featuring students from the Chola, Kakatiya, Maratha, and Mourya houses, each displaying unity and competitive spirit. The solemn moments of hoisting the school and National Flag were followed by mesmerizing classical dance performances and soulful musical renditions, highlighting the school's rich cultural diversity. Ananya Rao , newly appointed School Captain, administered the oath of office, pledging commitment to uphold school values. The event concluded on a patriotic note with the enthusiastic chant of "Bharat Mata Ki Jai," symbolizing unity and national pride. This ceremony not only celebrated student leadership but also reinforced the school's commitment to holistic education and cultural enrichment.

INTERNATIONAL YOGA DAY

Sri Prakash School and synergy academy Kakinada celebrated Yoga Day 2024 with an enriching session conducted by Mr. Ajith and Ms. Tulasi from the Isha Foundation, Kakinada. Students from grade VI to XII have attended the session. The event held in the presence of School Director Mr. Vijay Prakash, Principal Ms. Sreedevi and academic co-ordinator Mr. Asit Kumar Roy introduced students to basic yoga practices, including simple asanas, pranayama, and meditation techniques. These practices are known to improve flexibility, strength, and mental clarity, making them highly beneficial for students. Mr. Ajith and Ms. Tulasi highlighted the advantages of yoga in reducing stress, enhancing concentration, and promoting overall physical health. They emphasized that regular yoga practice can help students manage academic pressures and lead a balanced life. The instructors also introduced Inner Engineering; a holistic system developed by Sadhguru. This program aims to align the body, mind, emotions and energy, enabling students to achieve their full potential both academically and personally. Director Mr. Vijay Prakash gave an inspiring speech, emphasizing the importance of incorporating yoga into daily routines for overall well-being. The celebration was a great success, with Sri Prakash expressing heartfelt thanks to the Isha Foundation for their valuable contribution. Students left the event equipped with tools to enhance their well-being and academic performance.

Grand Inaugural of HARIVILLU -- Festivals Galore!

Amidst striking festoons, vibrant colors, multi-hued flowers, resplendent décor and ethnic vibes, festive spirit pervaded through the corridors of Sri Prakash Synergy School, Peddapuram as the grand inaugural of their two-day annual Art Expo HARIVILLU – festivals galore was held at 11: 30 a.m. on Saturday, 06 April 2024, in the august presence of distinguished guests. Shedding light on various genres of the two prominent areas of Art & Crafts and Music, the cultural carnival literally rolled out the red carpet for the guests, visitors, students, parents, and even general public to have a captivating experience of the aesthetics associated with the lavish theme of the event “National and International Festivals of Repute”. Around 20 exciting regional-specific festive themes were on spectacular display that ranged from Ganesh puja (Mumbai) to Onam (Kerala) to Bathukamma (Telangana), nationally, and from Abu Simbel (Egypt) to Rose Festival (Bulgaria) to Lantern Festival (Taiwan), internationally. A whopping 1200 Art & Craft aspirants animated the festive spirit in thousands of monumental art works whose unique origins, history, visual, and literary connotations, inter alia, evoked artistic emotions among the audience. On the other hand, the 90- minute Music Fest with around 200 music-savvy students harmonizing beautiful melodies across 12 captivating songs held the audience spellbound. Resembling a grand symphony orchestra, students lent glamour to their melodious voices. What marked as added features were the Viewer’s Quiz and the Treasure Hunt that ignited the imaginations of the guests and the general public alike. Smt Surabhi Vani Devi,MLC (Telangana State), founder of Sri Venkateswara Group of Institutions, and the proud daughter of former Prime Minister Bharat Ratna Sri P. V. Narasimha Rao graced the occasion as the Chief Guest. Dr. D. Ramaganesh, Consultant Pediatrician, Visakhapatnam, added great warmth to the expo as a Guest of Honor. Another Guest of Honor whose distinct presence added festive vibes was Smt Dhaassyam Geetha Bhascker, CEO, INDFAME, Trainer, Actress and the humble mother of famous film director Sri Tarun Bhascker. The Chief Guest, expressing her deep pleasure at the mammoth art expo, said that art is like a portrait which can express multiple emotions, and which doesn’t need the support any language to describe its essence. Complimenting the height of inventiveness that was abundantly exposed to the sublimity and grandeur of art & craft, she exhorted the students to sustain the creative- edge nurtured so that they could scale great heights in their lives and careers. Dr. D. Ramaganesh said he was bowled over by the impressive majesty of the art expo and further added that the Art Exhibition would undoubtedly add to the holistic development of students. Similarly, Smt Dhaassyam Geetha Bhascker lauded the school management for encouraging and inculcating such artistic values in the minds of students. She added that the contagiousness of creativity in unearthing artistry needed to be encouraged consistently. In his inaugural address, the school director Sri Ch. Vijay Prakash clarified that they have made Harivillu programme purely a theme- based one in order to represent the diverse features of Indian/international culture(s) – folk traditions, festivals, music, dance, paintings, ethos, ethics, etc. The director further added the credit for the grand success of the art expo was wholly due to the art & craft aspirants as they endeavored to accord perfect shape and structure to all the innovations. The stirring display of art & craft has been thrown open to the general public as well on both 6 and 7 April 2024 with a view to facilitating them to witness the scale of imaginativeness and inventiveness exhibited by the students. Senior Principal Sri M.V.V.S. Murthy, Professor S. R. Santhanam, Academic & Cultural Coordinators, Teaching and non-teaching staff, students, parents, and well-wishers were present.
